How to Hike

A guide to advanced walking.

  • Planning

    With great planning comes less responsibility.

    Hiking is about 98% passion. This is why having about 2% of a plan should carry you the rest of the way. Everything else is bonus.

  • Knowledge

    You either have it, or you google it.

    Let's face it, you probably googled us. That is unless you are a TA or instructor in charge of reading this assignment. In either case you know Knowledge is everything. That and you should just google what you don't know just to be sure. This also applys to Hiking & Stuff.

  • Gear

    How else would we make money?

    The most important part about a blog is a shameless plug. In this case we have to sell hiking gear in order to subsidize our dream of being passion bloggers instead of finding a job that is increadibly mondane and pays fairly well. So please by our gear and Go Prepared!

  • Survival

    You've succeded if you're here.

    At the end of the day if you completed a hike and survived you've done it! In some cases you might have a scary moment or two and those make for great campfire stories. If you have a super horric incident then Netflix might contract you to make a documentary or film about it, so I'd say that's a win too!

The Narrows

This is one of the most increadible hikes you can go on. So if you can, go.

The Narrows hike is iconic becuase you wade through water up a tall and, obviously, narrow canyon at the end of Zion National Park. You should bring, water shoes, hiking stick or pole, and quick dry clothing to ensure a safer and more comfortable hike.

  • Difficulty: Moderate to Difficult
  • Category: Bucket-list Worthy
